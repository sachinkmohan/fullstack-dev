# Resources — Problem Solving

Trusted sources for grounding lessons. Prefer these over parametric knowledge.

## Primary (high-trust, foundational)

- **George Pólya — *How to Solve It* (1945)** — The origin of the four-step problem-solving method (Understand → Plan → Carry out → Look back). Used everywhere from math to programming. Sold 1M+ copies, translated into 21+ languages.
  - Summary of the four phases: https://www.ms.uky.edu/~lee/ma310sp15/polya.pdf
  - Overview: https://study.com/academy/lesson/polyas-four-step-problem-solving-process.html

- **freeCodeCamp — "How to Solve Coding Problems with a Simple Four Step Method"** — Pólya's method adapted directly to programming, beginner-focused. https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/how-to-solve-coding-problems/

- **freeCodeCamp — "What is Pseudocode? How to Use Pseudocode to Solve Coding Problems"** — Why writing logic in plain language first reduces working-memory load. https://www.freecodecamp.org/news/what-is-pseudocode-in-programming/

- **The Odin Project — "Problem Solving"** — A practical, well-regarded walkthrough of understanding → planning → pseudocode for new developers. https://www.theodinproject.com/lessons/foundations-problem-solving

## The science of expertise (what makes great solvers great)

- **Peter Norvig — "Teach Yourself Programming in Ten Years" (2001)** — The canonical essay for *becoming a great programmer*. Source of the "deliberative practice" definition and the 10-item recipe (build, read others' code, work with/after other programmers, learn many paradigms). https://norvig.com/21-days.html

- **K. Anders Ericsson — "The Role of Deliberate Practice in the Acquisition of Expert Performance" (1993)** — The foundational research on deliberate practice: focused work at the edge of ability, with immediate feedback. https://www.ida.liu.se/~nilda08/Anders_Ericsson/Ericsson_delib_pract.pdf

- **Nokes, Schunn & Chi — "Problem Solving and Human Expertise" (2010)** — How experts differ from novices: pattern recognition, "chunks," deeper problem representation, faster qualitative reasoning. https://www.lrdc.pitt.edu/schunn/papers/Nokes,%20Schunn,%20&%20Chi,%202010.pdf

## Practice platforms (where to do the reps)

Match the platform to the *skill you're leveling*, and practice by **topic/pattern, not randomly**.

- **Exercism (JS/TS tracks)** — language fluency & idiomatic code, with *free human mentor review of your approach*. Best for "write it well," not just "make it pass." https://exercism.org/tracks/javascript
- **LeetCode** — data-structures & algorithms, interview-style. Use **by pattern**, not random. https://leetcode.com/
- **Sean Prashad — LeetCode Patterns** — free list grouping problems by underlying pattern (two pointers, sliding window, etc.). The cure for random grinding. https://seanprashad.com/leetcode-patterns/
- **NeetCode 150 / Roadmap** — curated, ordered DS&A path with video explanations. https://neetcode.io/practice
- **Codewars** — quick language-fluency katas (gamified). Fine as warm-ups, but pick focused sets, not random kata. https://www.codewars.com/
- **Frontend Mentor** — real frontend projects from professional designs (HTML/CSS/JS/React). https://www.frontendmentor.io/
- **build-your-own-x** — rebuild real software (a database, a React, a web server…) for deep full-stack understanding. https://github.com/codecrafters-io/build-your-own-x
- **roadmap.sh — Full-Stack** — community-trusted visual roadmap of the full-stack skill tree; good for spotting your gaps by stage. https://roadmap.sh/full-stack
