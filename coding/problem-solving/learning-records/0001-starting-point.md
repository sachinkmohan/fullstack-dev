# 0001 — Starting point

**Date:** 2026-06-17

## Context
Learner started the Problem Solving workspace after a Fibonacci problem they wrote "seemed not functional." Their question was explicitly *meta*: how to think about experimenting toward a solution, whether to use pen/paper or a digital medium, whether to write pseudocode, and how to "think to solve problems quickly."

## What we established (mission intake)
- **Goal:** general coding competence (not interview prep, not competitive programming).
- **Level:** Beginner+ — can write working code for simple tasks, but freezes when a problem feels new.
- **Language:** JavaScript / TypeScript.

## Key insight driving the curriculum
The learner's problem is not Fibonacci; it's the *absence of a process*. They jump from problem statement to typing code, skipping the understand/plan phases, so bugs are inevitable and confusing. The fix is a single repeatable loop (Pólya's four steps) practiced until automatic.

## Decisions
- Anchor the first lesson on Fibonacci itself, since it's their live pain point, but teach the *loop* as the transferable skill.
- Directly answer their pen/paper-vs-digital and pseudocode questions inside the loop (Understand & Plan are "thinking," not "typing").

## Next
Lesson 0001 — The four-step loop. After it, gauge whether to go deeper on "Understand" (examples & edge cases) or "Look back" (testing & reading errors).
