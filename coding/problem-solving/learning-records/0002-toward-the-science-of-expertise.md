# 0002 — Direction shift: toward the science of expertise

**Date:** 2026-06-17

## Context
After Lesson 1 (the four-step loop), the learner asked a deeper, reflective question:
*"What makes someone great at solving puzzles, only solving them? As a web dev, how could someone thrive and become the best?"*

This is a **wisdom + knowledge** question, not a request for another mechanical technique. It signals the learner cares about the long arc — what separates great problem-solvers from average ones, and how to get there as a web dev.

## What this means for the mission
The mission ("own a repeatable process; general web-dev competence") is unchanged, but the learner has revealed a stretch goal: not just *competent* but *excellent*. Lesson 2 reframes from "how to solve one problem" to "how the act of solving problems, repeated correctly, compounds into expertise."

## Key insights grounding Lesson 2
- Expertise is built, not born — via **deliberate practice** (Ericsson 1993): focused work just beyond current ability, with immediate feedback and error analysis.
- Experts aren't faster thinkers; they have larger **pattern libraries** ("chunks") — from the chess studies (Nokes/Schunn/Chi 2010). Each problem solved deliberately deposits a chunk.
- For web devs specifically, Norvig's recipe: build real things, read others' code, work *with* and *after* other programmers, learn multiple paradigms — over years.
- This connects back to Lesson 1: the four-step loop is *the rep*. Doing it deliberately is what makes reps count.

## Decisions
- Keep mission as-is; treat "become excellent" as a long-horizon thread.
- Lesson 2 = mindset + mechanism + a concrete personal practice plan, with a community recommendation (wisdom).

## Next
After Lesson 2, return to the skills track: Lesson 3 likely "Understand deeply — examples & edge cases" (the highest-leverage rep), so the loop has teeth before complexity/patterns.
